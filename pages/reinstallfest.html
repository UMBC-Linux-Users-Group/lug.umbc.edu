What is Re-Installfest?

Have you always wanted to try the latest and greatest Windows desktop operating system, but just couldn't escape the oppressive clutches of your slow, outdated GNU/Linux operating system? Well, now you finally can! The Linux Users' Group is working to provide installations of Windows 8.1 to users looking to move away from Linux operating systems, and into the warm, welcoming arms of Microsoft's most User-Friendly&tm; update to the Windows&tm; operating system yet! And, best of all, thanks to generous donations from people like you, we will even be covering the already-minimal cost of purchasing a license for this as well!

After this semester's InstallFest, we heard a lot of feedback from new Linux users, which eventually led us to the conclusion that we should offer a Re-InstallFest! We'll be collection donations to purchase the licenses needed, with a goal of at least 50 licenses. In the unlikely event that we are unable to purchase a sufficient number of licenses, we will begin charging admission on a first-come-first-served basis.

How do I get Windows 8.1 Installed? / How does this Work?

It Just Works&tm;*

If you want Windows 8.1, and want us to perform the installation, then you should come to Installfest.

Before Re-Installfest, you don't need to worry about backing up your important data. Windows&tm; knows best, and will preserve all of your files that it deems important enough in The Cloud&tm;.

At Installfest, you'll be asked to register. You may also try out our demo laptops, provided that you of course have agreed to the Microsoft Product Demonstration End User License Agreement beforehand, to help you get a better idea of the desktop environment that you will eventually learn to tolerate. If you haven't yet agreed for some reason, don't worry, there will be an opportunity to do so at the event! Prior to starting installation with one of our volunteers, you'll be asked to sign the Windows Software Re-Installation License Agreement, acknowledging that you agree to use exclusively Microsoft products in the future and that you will not install any communist software without the written permission of Bill Gates, as well as naming Microsoft, Inc. as your legal guardian in the event of any complications during the installation or any future use of the Windows 8.1 Operating System. Pretty standard stuff, really.
